## Design Context

### Users
Primary audience includes recruiters/hiring managers, potential freelance clients, and a broader developer audience evaluating technical depth.

This portfolio is used as a credibility surface during quick evaluations (hiring scans, intro calls, referrals, and profile checks). Visitors need to rapidly understand capability, quality of execution, and how to initiate contact.

### Brand Personality
Voice and tone should feel:
- Intelligent
- Scalable
- Adaptive

Emotional goals:
- Create immediate confidence and trust
- Signal ambition and forward momentum
- Communicate technical rigor without feeling generic or templated

### Aesthetic Direction
Direction: premium, confident, high-clarity personal brand with expressive but controlled motion.

Theme preference: both light and dark should be supported where practical.

Reference guidance: no explicit reference sites provided.

Anti-reference: avoid any look that feels AI-generic, template-like, or visually interchangeable.

### Design Principles
1. Communicate value fast: within the first viewport, users should understand strengths, focus areas, and a clear next action.
2. Distinctive over generic: use intentional typography, composition, and interaction details that feel authored, not auto-generated.
3. Performance-aware polish: motion and visual richness should reinforce hierarchy and never reduce responsiveness.
4. Accessibility by default: target WCAG 2.1 AA, respect reduced motion preferences, and avoid color-only meaning.
5. Trust through clarity: concise copy, strong information architecture, and visible proof of capability (projects, outcomes, contact paths).
